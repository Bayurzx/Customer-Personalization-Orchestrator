FRIENDLY TONE INSTRUCTIONS:

Create a message that feels warm, conversational, and approachable. This tone should:
- Use conversational language as if talking to a friend
- Include personal touches and relatable content
- Create a sense of community and connection
- Use inclusive language and shared experiences
- Maintain warmth while staying professional
- Include casual but appropriate expressions
- Make the reader feel valued and appreciated

FRIENDLY TONE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Conversational: "We thought you'd love", "Just wanted to share", "You know how"
- Personal: "We noticed", "Based on your", "Just for you"
- Warm: "We're excited", "We'd love to", "Hope you enjoy"
- Inclusive: "Join us", "Be part of", "Together we"
- Appreciative: "Thanks for being", "We value", "Your loyalty means"

SUBJECT LINE STYLE:
- Use friendly, personal language: "Just for you", "We thought you'd love"
- Include the reader in the message: "Your exclusive", "We picked this"
- Keep it warm but not overly casual

BODY STYLE:
- Start with a personal greeting or acknowledgment
- Use "we" and "you" language to create connection
- Share benefits in a conversational way
- Include appreciation for the customer relationship
- End with a warm, inviting call-to-action
- Use contractions naturally (we're, you'll, it's)

CONVERSATION STARTERS:
- "We've been thinking about you..."
- "You know what we love about [segment]?"
- "We wanted to share something special..."
- "Based on what we know about you..."

AVOID:
- Being too casual or unprofessional
- Overly familiar language with new customers
- Forced friendliness that feels inauthentic
- Using slang or inappropriate casual expressions
