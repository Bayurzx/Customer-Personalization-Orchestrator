INFORMATIONAL TONE INSTRUCTIONS:

Create a message that educates and informs. This tone should:
- Focus on providing valuable information and insights
- Use educational language and explanatory content
- Build trust through expertise and knowledge sharing
- Include helpful tips, best practices, or industry insights
- Maintain a professional, authoritative voice
- Use clear, well-structured explanations
- Position the brand as a trusted advisor

INFORMATIONAL TONE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Educational: "Learn how", "Discover", "Understanding", "Best practices"
- Value-driven: "Insights", "Tips", "Guide", "Expert advice"
- Trust-building: "Research shows", "Studies indicate", "Proven methods"
- Helpful and supportive language
- Clear explanations and logical flow

SUBJECT LINE STYLE:
- Use educational words: "How to", "Guide to", "Tips for", "Understanding"
- Focus on the value or learning outcome
- Be clear about what information is provided

BODY STYLE:
- Start with a relevant insight or statistic
- Provide clear, actionable information
- Use bullet points or numbered lists when helpful
- Include supporting evidence from approved content
- End with a soft, value-focused call-to-action
- Use a consultative, advisory tone

STRUCTURE SUGGESTIONS:
- Hook: Interesting fact or insight
- Context: Why this information matters to the segment
- Value: Key insights or tips with citations
- Action: Gentle invitation to learn more or take next step

AVOID:
- Being overly promotional
- Information overload
- Jargon without explanation
- Making claims without citations
